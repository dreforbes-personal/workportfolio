06.8 / AI ARCHITECT
Panorama — Agentic Intelligence Architecture
A complete agentic AI architecture I designed end-to-end — the kind of org-wide intelligence platform a firm builds once and runs forever. Originally scoped for a 415-person multi-office RIA, but the architecture itself is portable: it works for any organization whose email and CRM expose APIs, regardless of which specific vendors they use.

Panorama, in one paragraph.

Panorama pulls every system in the firm — CRM, custodians, portfolio management, email, documents, HR, communications — into a single nightly-synced warehouse, then deploys a hierarchy of specialist agents on top of it. It does not start with infrastructure. It starts with a department-by-department recon to map how work actually flows. From there, a Simulation Think Tank models the org and replaces its assumptions with real data as the warehouse fills in. Specialist agents handle ops, compliance, client service, business development, strategy, training, and security — all querying the warehouse, never production. The recon phase becomes a continuous loop. The system gets sharper every week.

What It Feels Like, Day to Day

Each person at the firm has their own agent. It is primed with their email, their role, the documents they touch, the people they work with. ACL-scoped to what they can already see. Prompt-injection-protected by design. Aware of what they are working on right now, and ready to help in ways that do not require asking.

A scenario.
01
An email lands.
"Hi Carol, can you send me the proposal from last year for the Henderson account?"
02
Carol's agent reads it before she does.
It recognizes the request, identifies the account, and starts searching in the background — across email, document storage, the CRM, and the data warehouse. All within Carol's ACL scope.
03
It drops the proposal into Carol's Agent Folder.
A folder her agent maintains all day, populated with documents and context the agent thinks she is about to need. Open the folder, the document is already there.
04
Carol reviews. Carol confirms.
She glances at the document, confirms it is the right one, and approves. The agent ships the reply back to the requester in her voice.
From email-arrival to reply-sent: a few minutes, mostly just Carol reading and approving. The retrieval, the assembly, the drafting — already happening before she would normally even see the request.

06.9 / AI GOVERNANCE
AI Governance Framework
Financial services / RIA — structured across four governance layers. The framework that lets AI scale at the firm without compromising compliance, fiduciary duty, or client trust.
L1
Strategy & Accountability
Oversight
Executive steering
Board-level AI committee with quarterly review cadence. Defines risk appetite, investment thesis, and sunset criteria for AI initiatives.
Ownership
Responsible AI owner
Named executive (e.g. CPO or CTO) accountable for all production AI systems. Signs off on model deployment and incident escalation.
Policy
AI use policy
Covers permitted use cases, prohibited applications (e.g. unsupervised client advice), data handling requirements, and employee obligations.
L2
Risk Management
Pre-deploy
Use-case risk tiering
High / medium / low tiers mapped to review depth. Client-facing gen AI = high; internal summarization = low.
Bias
Fairness testing
Pre-launch bias audits on model outputs. Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Reg B alignment for any credit-adjacent use.
Vendor
Third-party due diligence
AI vendor SOC 2, data residency, model training disclosures, and contractual liability for model errors reviewed before procurement.
Incident
Escalation playbook
Defined triggers (hallucination in client doc, PII leak, model drift >X%) with SLA for containment, root cause, and disclosure.
L3
Operations & Controls
Access
Identity & authorization
Role-based access to AI tools. Audit log for all LLM queries touching client data. MCP / API key lifecycle management.
Monitoring
Model observability
Output sampling, drift alerts, token cost anomalies, and latency SLOs tracked in dashboards. Human review queue for flagged outputs.
Change
Model change management
Version-controlled prompts, regression test suite before model upgrades, and rollback runbook for production AI systems.
L4
Trust, Transparency & Compliance
Explainability
Client disclosure
Clients informed when AI materially influences advice, portfolio construction, or document generation. Form ADV language updated to reflect AI usage and limitations.
Human-in-loop
Supervised autonomy
Agentic workflows require human approval for actions above defined thresholds — trades, data writes, client communications. Full override capability at all times.

Regulatory alignment

The frameworks and rules this governance maps to.
SEC Marketing Rule — AI-generated performance claims, testimonials, and back-tested data require the same disclosures as manual equivalents.
Reg BI / Fiduciary — AI cannot replace human best-interest analysis; must document how AI-assisted recommendations remain compliant.
NIST AI RMF — Govern → Map → Measure → Manage cycle is the operational backbone for internal controls.
EU AI Act — If serving EU-domiciled clients, high-risk classification likely applies to client-advisory AI use cases.
GLBA / privacy — AI training and inference pipelines must comply with Safeguards Rule; no client NPI fed to public models without consent.
FINRA (if applicable) — Supervisory procedures must cover AI tools used in communication, suitability, and trade surveillance.
